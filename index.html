📖 Glossary — Key Terms Explained
click to expand ▾
ETo (mm/month)
Reference evapotranspiration — water a well-watered grass field would use. The baseline from which all crop water needs are calculated. Source: Open-Meteo or CLIMDATA.
Kc (crop coefficient)
Multiplier that adjusts ETo for the specific crop and growth stage. Kc_ini (planting) → Kc_mid (peak) → Kc_end (harvest). Changes monthly as the crop grows.
RZD (root zone depth)
How deep the crop roots reach. Shallow at planting (0.2–0.3m), deeper at mid-season (0.6–1.5m). A deeper root zone stores more water — longer time between irrigations.
NIR (mm/month)
Net Irrigation Requirement — the water deficit the crop cannot get from rainfall. NIR = ETcrop − effective rainfall. This is the minimum water irrigation must supply.
IF (days) — Irrigation Frequency
Days between irrigation events. IF = available soil water ÷ daily crop demand. Short IF = irrigate often (high demand / sandy soil). Long IF = irrigate less often.
Ts (min) — Set Time
How long to run sprinklers at ONE position before moving. Ts = gross depth ÷ precipitation rate. Total irrigation time = Ts × number of shifts.
Q (m³/hr) — System Capacity
Flow rate the pump must deliver. Depends on area, gross depth, cycle length and hours per shift. Use this to select the pump size. Match to SunCulture pump max flow.
TDH (m) — Total Dynamic Head
Total pressure the pump must overcome: pipe friction + operating pressure + elevation difference. Use this to verify the pump can deliver water to the highest point.
Dnet / Dgross (mm)
Net depth = water the soil can hold above wilting point (WHC × RZD × depletion). Gross depth = Dnet ÷ efficiency. This is the depth applied each irrigation event.
WHC (mm/m) — Water Holding Capacity
How much plant-available water the soil holds per metre depth. Sandy soil: 50mm/m (drains fast). Clay soil: 150mm/m (holds more). Determines IF and Dnet.
Shift / Set
A sprinkler "set" or "shift" is one position of the lateral pipes. After Ts minutes the pipes are moved to the next position. Ns = number of shifts needed to cover the whole field.
Effective Rainfall
The portion of rainfall that actually enters the soil and is available to crops (typically 70% — the rest runs off or evaporates). Reduces irrigation requirement.

How to read this table: Each column is one week of the year (Week 1 = first 7 days of the month). Irrig. Weeks highlighted in amber are peak demand weeks — prioritise full application. Weeks in green have surplus rainfall — irrigation can be skipped or reduced.
